# Founder Vision

Internet Brain OS is being built as a serious company, not as a disposable side project.

## Founder intent

The company exists to build a living intelligence system that helps people investigate the public internet, retain knowledge, connect evidence, discover opportunities, and make stronger decisions.

The product should feel familiar, useful, and human. The final public name does not need to sound technical. Naming remains open until the product identity is mature.

## Ambition

The ambition is not to make a slightly better scraper.

The ambition is to create the intelligence layer people wish existed on top of the internet:

- A browser copilot that understands context.
- A research workspace that never loses knowledge.
- A second brain that becomes more useful after every search.
- A system of agents that can investigate, classify, connect, monitor, and report.
- A modular platform that grows through Skills and integrations.

## Economic reality

The company starts with limited capital.

This is a design constraint, not an excuse for weak architecture.

The system must therefore:

- Work locally where possible.
- Use free/open models first.
- Use expensive models selectively.
- Avoid mandatory cloud costs.
- Let users bring their own models and infrastructure.
- Grow through careful execution, not waste.

## What success looks like

A user should be able to express a goal such as:

> Find a promising product, identify suppliers, compare competition, estimate margins, preserve the evidence, and notify me when the opportunity changes.

The system should turn that goal into a structured Case, perform the work through agents and Skills, save the evidence, update memory, export to Obsidian, and present decisions rather than raw noise.

## Founder authority

The Founder owns final strategic approval.

AI agents may propose, investigate, implement, review, and document.

They may not silently redefine the company, change its ethical boundaries, or weaken the permanent project principles.

## CEO function

The strategic CEO function is responsible for:

- Protecting product coherence.
- Challenging weak assumptions.
- Reviewing major changes.
- Preventing scope drift.
- Turning ambitious ideas into controlled execution.

The operational orchestrator, such as Hermes, coordinates execution but does not own the permanent vision.

## Permanent statement

This repository is not only a software repository.

It is the institutional memory, operating doctrine, and evolving intelligence of the company.
