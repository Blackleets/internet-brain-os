# WOW Features

These concepts define the long-term product experience. They are not permission to skip the roadmap.

Every feature below must eventually be converted into a validated design, architecture decision, and scoped task before implementation.

## AI Browser Copilot

A quiet browser extension that understands the current public page and offers context-specific intelligence without flooding the user with notifications.

Examples:

- E-commerce page: margin, competition, supplier clues, trend, risk.
- Company page: technologies, growth signals, competitors, public leads.
- GitHub page: stack, license, dependencies, activity, public risks.

The extension is the interface. The Kernel performs the durable work.

## AI Overlay

An optional visual layer over public pages that displays compact scores, evidence, and actions directly in context.

It must remain controllable, non-intrusive, and transparent.

## Deep Public Research Mode

A thorough research mode that explores public and authorized sources such as:

- Search engines.
- Public archives.
- Open datasets.
- Public repositories.
- Public documents and PDFs.
- Public marketplaces.
- Public forums and social pages.
- RSS, sitemaps, and authorized APIs.

It never means bypassing authentication, paywalls, private systems, or access controls.

## Opportunity Finder

Converts research into ranked opportunities.

Examples:

- Products with plausible margin and manageable competition.
- Companies with visible service gaps.
- Suppliers with better economics.
- Markets showing early growth signals.

All estimates must expose assumptions, confidence, and evidence.

## Curiosity Engine

The system asks productive follow-up questions:

- Who else is connected?
- Why might this pattern exist?
- What evidence would confirm it?
- What has changed over time?

Curiosity must have budgets, stopping rules, and safety boundaries.

## Research Timeline

A replayable history of how a Case evolved:

- Sources discovered.
- Evidence captured.
- Hypotheses generated.
- Decisions made.
- Reports produced.

## Living Workspace

Each Case grows into an organized workspace containing:

- Evidence.
- Entities.
- Relationships.
- Hypotheses.
- Open questions.
- Risks.
- Reports.
- Exports.
- Monitoring rules.

## Internet Relationship Map

A graph connecting public entities such as:

Company -> Product -> Supplier -> Domain -> Technology -> Advertisement -> Competitor

Every relationship must point to evidence.

## Internet Time Machine

Compares historical snapshots when lawful and available:

- Price changes.
- Product additions/removals.
- Messaging changes.
- Technology changes.
- Campaign changes.

## Daily Brief and Eureka Moments

The system summarizes meaningful changes without notification spam.

Notifications should be classified:

- Informational: feed only.
- Important: quiet notification.
- Critical: immediate alert only when thresholds are met.
- Eureka: a new cross-case relationship or high-value insight with evidence.

## Skill Store

Modular Skills teach the Kernel how to work in specific domains.

Examples:

- Shopify Intelligence.
- Supplier Finder.
- Local Business Scanner.
- SEO Auditor.
- Competitor Tracker.
- PDF Intelligence.

## Agent and Workflow Marketplace

Long-term ecosystem for reusable:

- Skills.
- Agent teams.
- Workflows.
- Templates.
- Connectors.
- Dashboards.

The Kernel remains stable while capabilities expand.

## Brain Growth

The system should measure meaningful knowledge quality, not vanity metrics.

Possible measures:

- Evidence coverage.
- Decision traceability.
- Entity resolution quality.
- Documentation freshness.
- Reuse of prior knowledge.

## Core experience promise

The user should not feel that they installed a scraper.

They should feel that they gained a disciplined research team and a living second brain for the public internet.
